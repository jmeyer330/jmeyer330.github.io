Flexible Inorganic Thin Films via Controlled Spalling (in Progress)

As part of the ICORLAB Research group in the ECE department at UIUC, I am assisting with research in inorganic thin films for flexible electronics. I am using electron-beam deposition and electroplating to induce controlled-spalling in semiconductor wafers, a method to transfer off flexible films with superior electrical properties.

Coin-Audio Machine Learning Project (Summer 2017)

For this project I sound-proofed my basement and recorded the sound of a US coin dropping onto a ceramic tile. After getting 1000 audio samples of a Nickel, Penny, Quarter, or Dime dropping, I made a small program with the Python Librosa to quickly convert all audio files into spectrograms to be fed into a machine learning algorithm. In Nvidia Digits, I cut/fine-tuned a pre-designed machine learning algorithm, and was able to increase the identification rate of a coin based on its spectrogram from 55% to 76%.
